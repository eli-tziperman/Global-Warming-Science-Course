ABSTRACT:
Recent reconstructions of Mg and Ca concentrations of seawater 
indicate that seawater Mg/Ca changed significantly over the last 
5 million years (Ma).  Tropical sea surface temperature (SST) 
records for the last 5 Ma based on foraminiferal Mg/Ca 
paleothermometry assume constant seawater Mg/Ca.  These SST 
records suggest that average equatorial Pacific SSTs remained 
thermally stable from 5 to 2 Ma, after which significant cooling 
occurred only in the eastern equatorial Pacific. This study 
examines the implications of adjusting available equatorial Pacific 
SST records based on Mg/Ca paleothermometry to account for the 
inferred past variations of seawater Mg/Ca. The results suggest 
that both the cold and the warm regions of the equatorial Pacific 
were much warmer during the early Pliocene (30–31 °C), and that 
both regions experienced a marked cooling from ~4 Ma to ~1 Ma.  
This new interpretation of foraminiferal Mg/Ca creates a 
discrepancy with alkenone unsaturation-based SST records from 
the eastern equatorial Pacific, which might be due to either 
overestimation of changes in past seawater Mg/Ca or to factors 
affecting the interpretation of the UK'37 index. The adjusted 
SST records are consistent with the hypothesis that higher 
levels of greenhouse gases maintained the warmth of the 
early Pliocene.

DESCRIPTION: 
This study examines the implications of adjusting available 
equatorial Pacific SST records based on Mg/Ca paleothermometry 
to account for reconstructed past variations of seawater Mg/Ca 
(Fantle and DePaolo, 2006). The dataset contains interpolated 
unadjusted and adjusted Globigerinoides sacculifer Mg/Ca and 
SST data from ODP Holes 847 and 806B, in the eastern and 
western equatorial Pacific, respectively. The G. sacculifer 
Mg/Ca dataset used is from Wara et al., 2005, Science. 
All SSTs were calculated from eq. (8) in Dekens et al., 2002, G3:

806B SST = LN(Mg/Caforam/0.37)/0.09 + 0.36*(2.520) + 2

847 SST = LN(Mg/Caforam/0.37)/0.09 + 0.36*(3.373) + 2

G. sacculifer warm pool Mg/Ca ratios and SSTs agree with 
independent records based on the species Globigerinoides 
ruber (Medina-Elizalde and Lea, 2005, Science). 
Wara et al.,(2005) state that they also used the 
Dekens et al (2002)equations, but the published SSTs 
given in that paper are ~1 deg. Celsius warmer than the 
EPSL2008 calculation for reasons unknown.  

Data from Ocean Drilling Program (ODP) sites 847 and 806B:

ODP806B: 0°19.110'N, 159°21.660'W, 2519m depth.  
ODP847: 0°11.593'N, 95°19.227'W, 3334m depth.
